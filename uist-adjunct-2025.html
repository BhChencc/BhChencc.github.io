From Gentle Touch to Sharp Scratch: A Shape-Changing Haptic Interface Driven by Shape-Memory Alloy for Affective Touch

Baihui Chen, Ruyang Yu, Yujie Li, Rui Qi, Qiang Liu and Xueliang Li*
Publication: UIST Adjunct 2025
Abstract: Shape-changing haptic interfaces are increasingly used in research on affective responses in HCI. Despite recent advances, the pursuit of quieter and lighter modalities of shape-changing systems is critical for achieving diverse affective haptic experiences in various contexts. We present a Shape-Memory Alloy (SMA) driven interface, which can transition between four different haptic states: soft only, stiffened, spike protrusion, and 3-DOF spike motion. A preliminary evaluation of the system showed that most participants (6 out of 7) could distinguish these four states on the forearm area and reported particular affective responses associated with the interaction. This work demonstrates the potential of SMA for actuating quiet, lightweight shape-changing interfaces that provide diverse affective experiences. We hope our work could inspire future research on shape-changing systems in broader contexts, such as wearable devices and affective robots.
Keywords: Shape-Memory Alloy, Shape-Changing Interface, Affective Touch, Haptics
